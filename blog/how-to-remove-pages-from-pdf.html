How to Remove Pages from a PDF

Delete the pages you don't need — keep everything else

Got a PDF with blank pages, irrelevant sections, or pages that simply shouldn't be included when you share it? Removing specific pages from a PDF is one of those tasks that sounds simple but used to require paid software. Not anymore.

How to Remove Pages with SRJ Tools

Step 1: Open the Remove Pages tool.

Step 2: Upload your PDF. Each page is rendered as a thumbnail preview.

Step 3: Select the pages you want to delete.

Step 4: Click the remove button and download the cleaned-up PDF.

The result is a new PDF without the selected pages. The original page order is maintained for everything you keep.

Removing vs. Splitting — What's the Difference?

The Remove Pages tool deletes pages you don't want and gives you the rest. The Split PDF tool does the opposite — it extracts only the pages you select. Use whichever makes more sense for your situation. If you need 48 out of 50 pages, removing 2 is faster. If you need 3 out of 50, splitting is the way to go.

After Removing Pages

If the remaining document needs to be reorganized, the Organize PDF tool lets you drag and drop pages into a new order. And if the file is still too big after cleanup, compress it.
