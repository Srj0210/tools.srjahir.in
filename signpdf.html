About SRJ Tools — Sign PDF

Add your digital signature to any PDF document — using text or an uploaded image. Position your signature on specific pages with a live preview before saving.

How to Sign a PDF

Step 1: Upload the PDF you need to sign.

Step 2: Choose your signature type — type your name (rendered as a signature) or upload an image of your actual signature.

Step 3: Position the signature on the page. Adjust size and placement using the live preview.

Step 4: Select which pages should receive the signature.

Step 5: Click "Sign PDF" and download your signed document.

When to Use This

Signing contracts, agreements, consent forms, NOC letters, or any document that needs your signature. It's faster than printing, signing by hand, and scanning back — especially when you're working remotely or on a mobile device.

Common Questions

Q: Is this a legally valid digital signature?
This tool adds a visual signature overlay to the PDF. For legally binding digital signatures (with certificates), you may need specialized e-signature software depending on your jurisdiction.

Q: Can I sign multiple pages?
Yes, you can choose to apply your signature to specific pages or all pages.

After signing, you might want to password-protect the document or compress it before sending.
